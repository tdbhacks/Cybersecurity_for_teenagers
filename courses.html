Passwords

How to create a strong password

Step 0: Introduction

Here is a short video to understand why using a strong password is very important:


Step 1: the basics

Use uppercase letters, numbers and symbols

Use a mix of different types of characters (a, b, A, B, 1, 2, 3, !, &, *, $, etc.) to make the password harder to crack.

Length is important

Choose a password that’s long enough. There’s no minimum password length everyone agrees on, but you should generally go for passwords that are at least 8 characters long.

Avoid obvious substitutions

For example, “Passw0rd” isn’t strong just because you’ve replaced an o with a 0. That’s obvious and won't make your password any stronger.

Step 2: Try to randomize words

Stay away from obvious combinations of dictionary words. Any word on its own is bad. Any combination of a few words, especially if they’re obvious, is also bad. For example, “dog” is a terrible password. But “white dog” is also very bad.

Step 3: Add multiple layers of security

As described in the introductory video, try to use 2-step verification if your email provider, social network, or any other website provides it. This kind of verification requires you to use another device (such as your smartphone) as part of the login process. It's not impossible to crack, but it makes things way harder for hackers, since they would have to get access to your second device in order to hack your account.
