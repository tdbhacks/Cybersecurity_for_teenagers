How to spot malicious users, emails, and websites

Social Engineering and Phishing

Social Engineering is commonly referred to as the "art of hacking people". Basically, the attacker will try to exploit your misjudgment in order to gain sensitive information directly from you, instead of having to hack your devices. Here is an example of Social Engineering performed by an experienced professional against a cell phone provider:

As you just saw, Social Engineering can be a pretty powerful attack. Phishing can be considered one of its most popular branches, which specifically target users through emails and website popups. In order to protect yourself against this kinds of threats, always verify suspicious emails: if you get an email claiming that your social network account (or any other account) has been temporarily suspended, never call the number specified in it! Instead, go ahead and try to log into your account. If you successfully manage to login, then you know the email was a phishing attack. Otherwise, call the support number or send an email to the addres reported on the official website, NOT the one reported in the sketchy email, which is probably owned by the attacker. Also, NEVER SEND YOUR PASSWORD TO ANYONE. Tech companies will never ask you to do that, since they usually require you to reset it or use some other kind of verification.
